Syllabus

Climate Change Governance: Science, Data & Models · Fall 2025–26

Course Description

Short version: This interdisciplinary course equips students with the tools, models, and frameworks necessary to understand and influence climate, water, energy, and food policy through data-driven systems thinking.

Long version: This interdisciplinary course equips students with the tools, models, and frameworks necessary to understand and influence climate, water, energy, and food policy through data-driven systems thinking. It blends technical modeling with governance insights into climate adaptation, sustainable agriculture, and infrastructure resilience. Students engage in scenario-based planning, explore socio-technical systems, and work with real-world case studies from the Indus Basin. Across fourteen weeks, the course moves from the physical science basis of climate change through the international and national policy landscape, into hands-on work with scenario tools and integrated assessment models across the energy, water, and agriculture sectors, culminating in a nexus-focused capstone project.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

CLOOutcome
CLO1Explain the physical drivers of climate change and key metrics.
CLO2Critically assess climate policy and reporting frameworks.
CLO3Apply computational modeling and data visualization tools to develop pathways for energy, water, and agriculture systems.
CLO4Interpret and communicate complex scenario results to technical and non-technical audiences.
