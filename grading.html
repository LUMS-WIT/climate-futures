Rubrics

Class Engagement — 20%

Class Engagement is assessed on the basis of active participation rather than attendance, including questions raised during lecture, written responses to one-minute papers, and contributions to paper discussions. The evaluation criterion is evidence that assigned readings were completed and critically evaluated, not the frequency of participation.

Strong: Consistently raises questions grounded in the assigned readings; contributes substantively to paper discussions; one-minute papers demonstrate synthesis rather than summary.
Adequate: Responds appropriately when called upon; one-minute papers indicate that the assigned reading was completed.
Limited: Passive participation; one-minute papers are superficial, generic, or absent.

Assignments — 10%

Assignments are take-home. Use of generative AI tools is permitted, provided that the prompts used are submitted alongside the completed assignment, which allows evaluation of the reasoning process in addition to the final output. Assignments demonstrating critical engagement with an AI tool — for example, identifying and correcting errors in its output, or iteratively refining a query — are evaluated according to different criteria than assignments completed without AI assistance; neither approach is penalized by default. In both cases, the assessment criterion is demonstrated understanding of the submitted work.

Midterm & Final Exams — 20% each

Both examinations are open-book. The format — written, oral, problem-based, or a combination — is determined by the instructor and announced in advance of each examination; the format of the midterm and final examination may differ. In all cases, examinations require live reasoning through course material rather than recall or recognition.

Project — 30%

Milestones and defense format are detailed on the Project page. Grading evaluates both the team-produced artifact and each student's individual defense of that artifact; the precise weighting between these components will be announced with the milestone rubric early in the semester.

Course Philosophy on AI Use

Generative AI tools are permitted for take-home assignments on the condition of full disclosure: prompts used must be submitted alongside the completed work. This requirement exists to make the reasoning process visible for evaluation, not merely the final output. Assignments are not evaluated on whether AI was used, but on whether the submitted work demonstrates genuine understanding; the evaluation criteria for AI-assisted and non-AI-assisted work differ accordingly, reflecting the different evidence available in each case.

Class Engagement, both examinations, and the project defense are conducted synchronously and in person, where AI assistance is not available. These components provide a direct assessment of individual understanding.

Policies

Academic Honesty

LUMS maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward academic dishonesty. Students are responsible for upholding academic integrity; where ambiguity exists, students should consult the student handbook or contact the instructor or teaching assistant in advance of submission. Use of AI on an assignment without disclosure of prompts, as specified above, constitutes a violation of this policy. Essays may be checked for similarity via similarity@lums.edu.pk prior to submission.

Generative AI Policy

The application of this policy within the course is described under Course Philosophy on AI Use, above. The underlying university-level policy: AI tools are permitted for brainstorming, refining a research question, locating information, drafting an outline, and checking grammar and style. They are not permitted for submitting AI-generated content as one's own in discussion posts or live sessions, for completing group work assigned to an individual student, or for producing any part of a graded submission — from outline through final draft — without disclosure. Violations are treated as academic misconduct.

Accommodations

Long-term medical conditions are accommodated through the Office of Accessibility & Inclusion (OAI). Short-term emergencies affecting coursework are handled by the instructor or Student Support Services (SSS); students should make contact directly and as early as possible, prior to the relevant deadline.

Harassment-Free Classroom

This class is a harassment-free environment. Behavior that causes discomfort or undermines a student's standing in the class will not be tolerated. Harassment experienced or observed should be reported to the instructor directly, or to OAI at oai@lums.edu.pk or harassment@lums.edu.pk.
