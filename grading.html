Rubrics

Class Engagement — 20%

This isn't attendance. It's whether you're thinking out loud in the room — asking a real question, writing a sharp one-minute paper at the end of a session, pushing back in a paper discussion. We're looking for evidence you did the reading and are testing it against what you already know, not whether you spoke the most.

Strong: consistently raises questions grounded in the reading, contributes to paper discussions, one-minute papers show synthesis, not summary.
Adequate: present and responsive when called on; one-minute papers show the reading was done.
Weak: passive presence; one-minute papers thin, generic, or missing.

Assignments — 10%

Take-home. You can use AI — but you'll submit the prompts you used alongside your answer, so we can see your thinking, not just your output. An assignment that shows a sharp, critical back-and-forth with an AI tool (you pushing it, catching its mistakes, refining the question) is evaluated differently than one written without AI — not automatically penalized, but held to a different bar for what "understanding this" looks like. Either way, what's graded is whether you understand what you turned in.

Midterm & Final Exams — 20% each

Both are open-book. Format — written, oral, problem set, or a mix — is set closer to the date and announced in class by the instructor; it may differ between the midterm and the final. What stays constant: you need to reason through the material live, with your notes in front of you, not just recognize it.

Project — 30%

See the Project page for milestones and defense format. Grading covers both what the team produces (the artifact) and how well each of you can defend it individually — the exact split will be announced with the milestone rubric early in the semester.

Course Philosophy on AI Use

Take-home assignments are where you're allowed to use AI — and expected to be transparent about it. Submit the prompts you used alongside your answer. We're not grading whether you used AI; we're grading whether you understood what you turned in, and a transcript of a sharp, critical back-and-forth with a model is itself evidence of that. An assignment built with AI and one built without it are evaluated differently — not because one is penalized, but because the bar for "did you understand this" looks different depending on how you got there.

Everything else — Class Engagement, both exams, and your project defense — happens live, in the room, where AI can't help you. That's on purpose: it's where we actually find out what you know.

Policies

Academic Honesty

LUMS has zero tolerance for academic dishonesty. You're responsible for upholding academic integrity — if you're unsure where a line is, check the student handbook or ask the instructor or TA before, not after. Using AI on an assignment without disclosing your prompts, as described above, is a violation, not just a style choice. Essays can be checked for similarity via similarity@lums.edu.pk before submission.

Generative AI Policy

See Course Philosophy on AI Use above for how this plays out in this course specifically. The university-level policy underneath it: AI tools are permitted for brainstorming, refining a research question, finding information, drafting an outline, and checking grammar and style. They are not permitted for submitting AI-generated content as your own in discussion posts or live sessions, for completing group work assigned specifically to you, or for writing any part of a graded submission — outline through final draft — without disclosure. Violations are treated as academic misconduct.

Accommodations

Long-term medical conditions are handled through the Office of Accessibility & Inclusion (OAI). Short-term emergencies that affect your coursework go through the instructor or Student Support Services (SSS) — talk to me directly and early, not after a deadline has passed.

Harassment-Free Classroom

This class is a harassment-free zone. No behavior that makes someone uncomfortable or undermines their standing in the class will be tolerated. To report harassment experienced or observed, contact me directly, or OAI at oai@lums.edu.pk / harassment@lums.edu.pk.
