# Climate Change Governance: Science, Data & Models

**ENGG562/ENV462**, LUMS, Fall 2025–26. An interdisciplinary course on climate, water, energy, and food policy through data-driven systems thinking — blending physical climate science, governance frameworks, and hands-on scenario modeling.

Live site: https://lums-wit.github.io/climate-futures/
Repo: [lums-wit/climate-futures](https://github.com/lums-wit/climate-futures)

## Course Objectives

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Explain the physical drivers of climate change and key metrics (radiative forcing, carbon budgets, etc.).
2. Critically assess climate policy and reporting frameworks (NDCs, carbon pricing, mitigation/adaptation).
3. Apply computational modeling and data visualization tools to develop pathways for energy, water, and agriculture systems.
4. Interpret and communicate complex scenario results to technical and non-technical audiences.

The course runs 14 weeks across 7 modules — climate science foundations, policy landscape, scenarios/IAMs, and sector deep-dives into energy, water, and agriculture — culminating in a nexus-focused capstone project grounded in Indus Basin case studies. Full breakdown in [schedule.html](schedule.html) and [syllabus.html](syllabus.html).
