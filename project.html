Capstone Project

Project details below are indicative and will be finalized early in the semester.

Overview

Placeholder — teams will apply scenario tools and integrated assessment approaches introduced in the course (Scenario Explorer, WRI Aqueduct, FABLE, Climate Action Simulation) to a real-world water–energy–food nexus problem, with emphasis on the Indus Basin context. Full project brief to be posted here.

Teams

Placeholder — students will work in teams of 3–4, formed by Week 4. Team formation guidelines and role expectations (research lead, modeling lead, policy lead, communications lead) will be posted here.

Timeline

  • Week 4 — Team Formation Teams finalized; initial topic areas proposed.
  • Week 6 — Proposal One-page project proposal defining scope and chosen sector(s).
  • Week 10 — Progress Check-in Interim findings and preliminary modeling results reviewed.
  • Week 13 — Policy Brief Draft policy brief workshopped in class.
  • Week 14 — Capstone Presentations Final presentations delivered across both lectures.

Deliverables

Project Proposal
One-page scope document, Week 6.
Policy Brief
2–3 page written brief translating findings into policy options.
Final Presentation
15-minute team presentation with Q&A, Week 14.
Technical Appendix
Data, model settings, and analysis supporting the brief.

Grading Rubric

Capstone/research project rubric — condensed. Full rubric to be distributed with the project brief.

Dimension Excellent (A+/A) Moderate (C- to B+) Inadequate (D/F)
Problem Definition & Relevance Focused question tied to course goals and local/regional context; measurable scope. Relevant but broad; scope creep risks. Tangential to course aims; no clear problem.
Methods & Modeling Appropriate tool choice (e.g., Scenario Explorer, FABLE); sound assumptions; transparent workflow. Applies tools but with gaps or limited justification. Inappropriate or unjustified methods; no credible methodology.
Governance Linkages Translates results into concrete policy options; trade-offs identified. Mentions policy links but shallow. No clear policy translation.
