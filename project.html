Capstone Project

How to Approach Your Project

Project selection should be based on genuine intellectual interest rather than convenience. Students should identify a question in energy, water, agriculture, or the interactions among these sectors that merits sustained investigation.

The research question should be formulated before a method or tool is selected. A well-posed question is falsifiable: by the conclusion of the project, it should be possible to state whether the evidence supports the question, contradicts it, or leaves it unresolved.

The choice of method should follow from the question rather than precede it. Appropriate methods include integrated assessment models (MESSAGEix, GCAM, or comparable frameworks), land-use tools such as FABLE, decision-science frameworks such as Robust Decision Making (the XLRM framework: Uncertainties, Levers, Relationships, Metrics), or alternative approaches including systems mapping, comparative policy analysis, or original data analysis. Methodological fit is the objective, not technical complexity for its own sake.

The project should conclude by returning to the original research question: what was found, the degree of confidence in the finding, and what evidence would alter the conclusion.

A subset of projects each year is selected for publication on this website as short public briefs.

Previous Projects

The following table lists project titles from a previous cohort, illustrating the range of scope and methodology represented in past work, including desk-based policy review, applied integrated assessment modeling, and governance analysis. These examples are provided as a reference for calibrating project scope and are not intended for direct replication.

# Title Plain-Language Description Technical Description Reference Project Nature
1 NDC Pakistan Review Pakistan submitted its updated Nationally Determined Contribution to the UNFCCC. This project asks whether that commitment is realistic, fair, and strong enough to cut emissions and protect the country's future. Critically review Pakistan's updated NDC. Examine how projected emissions are calculated, what assumptions are made, and whether the targets align with long-term mitigation pathways. Can include engagement with the Ministry of Climate Change and desk-based research. NDC3.0 Pakistan Desk review / synthesis
2 Review of Pakistan's Climate Governance Structures Maps the governance structure behind Pakistan's climate policies, and identifies what's missing — where different announcements and legislation contradict each other. Conduct a comprehensive review of climate policies across energy, transport, agriculture, and industry. Identify gaps, overlaps, and opportunities for alignment with resilience and net-zero goals. Pakistan Climate Change Policy 2021 Desk review / synthesis
3 IPCC Reports Review Pick a chapter or group of chapters from the IPCC AR6 reports and do a deep dive. For example, if transport transitions interest you, find the transport-focused chapters and work through a group of scenarios. Select a chapter (e.g., AR6 WGII — Impacts, WGIII — Mitigation, or WGI — Physical Science). Summarize and synthesize the main insights. Develop your own understanding of how the chapter connects to a particular region or global challenge. IPCC AR6 Reports Desk review / synthesis
4 Climate Impact Explorer Case Study An online tool that shows how climate change affects rainfall, floods, and temperature. This project uses it to show what different regions of the world could face under 1.5°C, 2°C, or 3°C of warming. Use the Climate Impact Explorer to develop a case study for a chosen country or region. Analyze different climate indicators (temperature, rainfall, droughts, floods) and synthesize how impacts evolve under various warming levels. Climate Impact Explorer Analytical / case study
5 Climate Risk Dashboard Case Study A tool that highlights which countries or sectors are most at risk from climate disasters. Choose a sector or region and build a storyline around a set of scenario combinations. Develop a case study using IIASA's Climate Risk Dashboard. Assess risk for a country or sector by interpreting climate hazard, exposure, and vulnerability indicators. Compare across regions or policy contexts. Climate Risk Dashboard Analytical / case study
6 Climate Solutions Explorer Case Study An online explorer of possible climate solutions. See the Net Zero Stories in the explorer, then try to build a similar net-zero story for a country the tool is missing. Explore low-carbon solutions using the Climate Solutions Explorer. Select a sector or technology and summarize potential mitigation impacts, trade-offs, and policy relevance. See the Net Zero Stories tab for a working example. Climate Solutions Explorer Analytical / case study
7 Short-Term Policy Measures and Emissions Pathways in Pakistan Uses Pakistan's integrated assessment model (MESSAGEix) to test "what if" scenarios — e.g., what happens if Pakistan adds a new clean-energy policy today and sets targets for 2035. Use Pakistan's NDC and current-policy (CurPol) scenarios. Select one sector (power or transport), implement an additional short-term policy (e.g., ethanol blend, solar thermal), and compare results against baseline and NDC trajectories. NDC3.0 Pakistan Analysis — MESSAGEix support available
8 Equity and Effort-Sharing in National Climate Pathways Also uses Pakistan's IAM, to ask whether Pakistan's targets are fair compared to other countries — measured against principles like responsibility, capability, or income level. Based on Dekker et al. (2020). Run the LTS scenario, implement effort-sharing bounds (per-capita, convergence, ability-to-pay, equal cumulative per capita), and compare. Assess the fairness of Pakistan's NDC, the trade-offs involved, and the implied net-zero year. Dekker et al. (2020) Analysis — MESSAGEix support available
9 Agrivoltaics Potential for the Indus Basin Food-Water-Energy Nexus Agrivoltaics means growing crops under solar panels. This project asks how it could help Pakistan save water, grow food, and generate clean energy at the same time. Explore how agrivoltaics (solar + crops) can support food-water-energy security in the Indus Basin. Provide an outlook to 2050. FAO Agrivoltaics Brief Literature + conceptual modeling
10 Agrivoltaics for Groundwater Depletion Mitigation Explores how solar panels on farmland could reduce overuse of groundwater while still supporting crops. Assess agrivoltaics potential across agroecological zones in Pakistan. Evaluate groundwater-depletion mitigation under different crop/climate scenarios. World Bank Groundwater Report Literature + conceptual modeling
11 Independent Assessment Using the IPCC AR6 Scenario Explorer An online database of climate scenarios. This project compares different regions' energy and emissions futures and explains what they mean. Use the IPCC Scenario Explorer to analyze global or regional pathways. Conduct a mini-assessment (e.g., regional energy mix, global emissions) and compare across regions. AR6 Scenario Explorer Analytical / data-based
12 FABLE-Pak: Diet Shifts Uses the FABLE calculator to test how eating less meat or more vegetables in Pakistan would affect farmland use, food supply, and emissions. Explore the effects of dietary changes on food demand, land use, and GHG emissions in Pakistan. FABLE Calculator Analysis — FABLE support available
13 FABLE-Pak: "Calisto Doomsday" Scenario A "what if" scenario in FABLE: what happens if Pakistan pushes farms to maximum output but harms biodiversity and resilience in the process. Design and simulate a scenario where agro-productivity is maximized at the cost of biodiversity and resilience. Introduce breaking points (e.g., species-extinction thresholds). FABLE Calculator Analysis — FABLE support available
14 FABLE-Pak: Forests vs. Fields In FABLE, test what happens if Pakistan grows more forests instead of expanding farms — balancing biodiversity, emissions, and food needs. Analyze how restricting cropland expansion while increasing afforestation commitments influences biodiversity, emissions, and food security. FABLE Calculator Analysis — FABLE support available
15 HTE Networks in Climate Policy Looks at how people, technology, and environment interact within a sector. The project proposes one change (like EV adoption) and maps how it ripples through the system. Use the Human–Technical–Environmental (HTE) framework to analyze a non-FWE sector (transport, urban, waste, industry). Identify key human actors, technologies, and environmental elements. Propose one intervention and show how it "rewires" the system. Siddiqi, Wescoat & Selin (2024) Governance / systems analysis
16 Climate Change and International Cooperation Uses the Indus Waters Treaty as an example to study how climate change might affect cooperation between India and Pakistan over shared rivers. Use the 1960 Indus Waters Treaty as a case study. Explore how climate change may affect regional cooperation (data-sharing, drought/flood response, adaptation). Compare Pakistan's NDC/adaptation needs with existing cooperative mechanisms. Yoshihide et al. (2019) Governance / case study
17 Smallholders, Vulnerability, and Climate Governance Examines the extent to which small farmers are supported by climate policy, with a focus on heat, water shortages, and equity in government strategy. Assess how climate governance addresses (or neglects) smallholder farmers' vulnerabilities: heat stress, water scarcity, livelihoods. Examine national adaptation plans, provincial strategies, or local schemes. Identify equity–resilience trade-offs. Batool & Saeed (2018) Governance / equity focus
